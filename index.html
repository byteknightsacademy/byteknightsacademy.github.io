FAQ's

What is Byte Knights?

▾

Byte Knights is a coding tutoring service that teaches kids programming concepts through engaging, project-based lessons in a fun, medieval-themed learning environment. Our goal is to help young learners become skilled coders, or "Byte Knights."

What Programming Languages Do We Teach?

▾

Our Head Knights are specialized in both Python and Java.

What Are Our Qualifications?

▾

Our tutors received a perfect score on the College Board AP Computer Science A Exam, ranked academically in the top 3% of their high school class, scored 1500+ on the SAT, and previously taught 50+ students.

What Level Of Experience is Required To Start?

▾

No prior coding experience is required! Byte Knights is designed for both beginners and those with some foundational knowledge who want to enhance their skills.

How is the Curriculum Structured?

▾

Our lessons are module-based, with each module representing a specific coding concept that students must "overcome" to advance through the ranks of the Byte Knights. These lessons are project-based and designed to teach both fundamental coding concepts and develop problem-solving skills.

How Are Sessions Structured?

▾

Each session begins with 10-15 minutes dedicated to answering any questions about the previous week's topic. This is followed by a 15-20 minute quiz to assess understanding. Afterward, another 10-15 minutes are spent on the current lesson, and the remaining time is focused on hands-on work, allowing students to apply the concepts they've learned.

How Do We Communicate?

▾

We keep parents and students informed through regular updates, including progress reports, project summaries, and quiz results. We also communicate important information, such as schedule changes or upcoming events, via email or on our social media! For more detailed inquiries or feedback, parents can reach out directly to us through our website’s contact form or by email.

How Do I Pay?

▾

After registering your child/children through our website, payment information will be sent out to you via email.
